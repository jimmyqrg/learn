Web Memes

Explaining 30 influential internet memes from 2024 to now

1. “Selling”

Origin & Background. The meme “Selling” emerged from gaming and livestream communities, where viewers humorously accused players of “selling” a match—meaning performing so poorly it appears deliberate. The phrase expanded across TikTok and YouTube, evolving into a shorthand critique for any conspicuously bad performance, mistake, or failure in judgment.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. In contemporary usage, “selling” operates as a light-hearted indictment of underperformance. It can target academic errors, social missteps, or ironic self-commentary. The term often circulates in high-energy digital subcultures where exaggeration, competitive framing, and performative critique are normalized modes of engagement.

Impact & Interpretation. The meme’s popularity underscores how everyday competence is gamified within online environments. Calling someone “selling” reframes error as spectacle, turning ordinary fallibility into a participatory joke. It reveals how digital communities construct micro-languages that balance accountability, humor, and social bonding.

2. “Cook / You’re Cooking”

Origin & Background. “Cook” arose from African American Vernacular English (AAVE), where it historically meant performing exceptionally well. The term was later popularized by sports commentary and spread widely across TikTok and Twitter, eventually losing its niche identity and becoming a mainstream expression of high praise.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. When someone is “cooking,” they are excelling with a level of creativity, mastery, or intensity that appears effortless. The compliment applies to jokes, academic insight, artistic work, or even emotional intelligence. It flips the negative framing of “selling” into a celebratory acknowledgment of peak performance or charismatic delivery.

Impact & Interpretation. The rise of “cook” illustrates how digital lexicons quickly absorb and repurpose linguistic markers from cultural communities. Its spread demonstrates social media’s tendency to elevate vivid metaphors—like cooking—as expressive shorthand for competence and flair. In doing so, it highlights the performative nature of online identity construction.

3. Skibidi Toilet

Origin & Background. Skibidi Toilet originated from the YouTube creator DaFuq!?Boom!, who released a surreal CGI series featuring human heads emerging from toilets battling humanoid camera-headed entities. The visuals combined absurdist humor, hyper-edited pacing, and unconventional lore-building, enabling the series to proliferate rapidly across youth-centered digital platforms.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. The meme embodies the resurgence of maximalist, chaotic internet humor—imagery purposefully distant from traditional narrative logic. For many viewers, its appeal lies in its unpredictability, distorted world-building, and exaggerated audiovisual style. It is frequently referenced in short-form videos, parodies, and remix cultures that thrive on rapid contextual mutation.

Impact & Interpretation. Skibidi Toilet demonstrates how contemporary meme culture rewards sensory excess and deliberately irrational storytelling. Its viral trajectory reflects a generational shift toward absurdist digital aesthetics that challenge conventional media norms. The franchise functions as both parody and spectacle, illustrating how internet communities co-create meaning by amplifying shared surrealism.

1. Tralalero Tralala

Origin & Background. "Tralalero Tralala" began as a short vocal riff that circulated on short-form video platforms where creators layered it over unexpected or absurd visuals. The sound's repetitive hook and light melodic structure made it easy to synchronize with edits, enabling large-scale adoption.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. Users apply the sound as an aural signifier of whimsy or to punctuate a sudden shift in context; when attached to otherwise ordinary recordings, the vocalization reframes the moment as humorous. Its simplicity invites rapid remixing across cultural and linguistic boundaries.

Impact & Interpretation. The meme underscores contemporary memetics' emphasis on audio-visual coupling: short audio hooks function as memetic affordances, increasing both recognition and spread. Scholars observe that such sounds facilitate communal participation and lower barriers to creative iteration.

2. NPC Meme

Origin & Background. The NPC meme draws visual and conceptual inspiration from video games where non-playable characters exhibit scripted behavior. It emerged in political and cultural discourse to satirize perceived predictability in public opinion or mass behavior.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. As a rhetorical device, the NPC label is used to critique or caricature individuals or groups who appear to echo scripted talking points or predictable online reactions. Memes often employ monochrome imagery and captioned speech bubbles to convey the metaphor.

Impact & Interpretation. Academically, NPC memes illustrate how gaming metaphors travel into civic conversation, offering researchers a lens for studying how digital idioms shape social judgment. The format's participatory nature also fosters iterative critique and subversive remixing.

3. Cheems

Origin & Background. Cheems uses a photograph of a Shiba Inu paired with intentional misspellings—commonly extra "m" insertions—to signal a certain awkwardness or endearing incompetence. The meme's iconography has been sustained by its expressive canine face and textual play.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. Creators utilize Cheems to express self-effacing humor, social hesitation, or minor anxieties. The format often juxtaposes an unconfident Cheems statement with a more assertive counterpart (e.g., Doge), producing a relational comedic effect.

Impact & Interpretation. From an interpretive standpoint, Cheems demonstrates how anthropomorphism and linguistic alteration combine to produce affective resonance. Its persistence suggests that visual cuteness and textual idiosyncrasy are potent vectors for emotional identification in digital humor.

4. Cursed Images

Origin & Background. Cursed images are photographs or composites designed to unsettle, amuse, or provoke through incongruity. The practice rose from communities that prized the dissonant and the uncanny as sources of ironic engagement.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. Audiences circulate cursed images for shock value and communal commentary; these images often function as prompts for captioning, remixing, or curatorial humor. Their ambiguous affect—between disgust and amusement—drives sustained engagement.

Impact & Interpretation. Analyzing cursed images reveals trends toward absurdism and anti-narrative aesthetics in online humor. They challenge normative expectations of visual coherence, thereby creating layered experiences of discomfort, humor, and collective interpretation.

5. TikTok Sound Remixes

Origin & Background. Short audio excerpts on TikTok become memetic when users repeatedly remix them into new contexts; these sounds range from musical loops to spoken lines that are repurposed as comedic or expressive markers.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. Sound remixes operate as memetic templates: creators map new narratives onto the same auditory scaffold, enabling rapid cultural transmission. This modality privileges rhythm, timing, and audio-driven recognition in digital communities.

Impact & Interpretation. The prevalence of sound memes underscores the centrality of auditory culture in contemporary memetics. They amplify participatory creativity and foreground multimodal meaning-making—where audio cues restructure visual content's interpretive frame.

6. Wojak Variants

Origin & Background. Wojak, a minimalist drawn figure, serves as a flexible vehicle for expressing affective states, from melancholic introspection to ironic triumph. Over time, numerous variants have multiplied to convey nuanced emotional registers.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. Variants (e.g., Doomer, Bloomer) create typologies of experience—each Wojak iteration embodies a sociocultural stance or personal disposition. These variants often appear in serialized comics or comparison formats to explore identity and sentiment.

Impact & Interpretation. Wojak's adaptability illustrates how simple visual prototypes enable rich symbolic labor. Scholars point to its role in constructing pseudo-autobiographical narratives within meme communities, facilitating empathetic recognition and discourse on modern anxieties.

7. "Ight, Imma Head Out" (SpongeBob)

Origin & Background. This meme uses a still of SpongeBob standing up as he prepares to leave, captioned to indicate withdrawal from awkward or undesirable situations. Its recognizable visual allowed it to travel quickly across social media.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. Creators apply the image to annotate moments warranting rapid exit—socially awkward scenarios, regrettable choices, or comedic avoidance. The concision of the template makes it effective for quick, widely comprehensible commentary.

Impact & Interpretation. The SpongeBob-based meme exemplifies how established media objects become semantic shortcuts in online discourse, enabling instant recognition and empathetic alignment through shared pop-cultural references.

8. Galaxy Brain

Origin & Background. Galaxy Brain visualizes escalating cognitive states via images of progressively activated brains, often climaxing with cosmic imagery. Initially a satirical depiction of supposed intellectual depth, it has been widely parodied.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. The meme is used both earnestly and ironically: to highlight genuinely sophisticated reasoning or to lampoon pretentiousness. Its tiered structure enables stratified rhetorical comparison across ideas or behaviors.

Impact & Interpretation. Galaxy Brain functions as a meta-commentary on discursivity and intellectual posturing; it reveals how visual metaphors can compress evaluative hierarchies into compact, shareable templates that drive discursive circulation.

9. Stonks

Origin & Background. The Stonks meme features a stock-market caricature and an upward-trending arrow to satirize simplistic or illogical economic optimism. It emerged from parodying amateurish financial reasoning.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. Users employ Stonks to criticize poor financial choices, oversimplified market commentary, or to humorously celebrate dubious successes. The visual shorthand communicates both irony and a playful skepticism toward market narratives.

Impact & Interpretation. Stonks highlights how memes can serve as an accessible critique of complex systems like finance, translating economic discourse into vernacular humor and enabling broad social commentary on risk, value, and perception.

10. Doge / Cheems Relations

Origin & Background. Doge—featuring a particular Shiba Inu photograph with broken English captions—preceded Cheems and shaped early 2010s meme culture. Subsequent pairings of Doge and Cheems construct dynamic contrasts.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. Juxtapositions between Doge and Cheems create dialogic humor: Doge's confident simplicity opposes Cheems' hesitant voice, enabling commentary on competence, bravado, and self-image within internet communities.

Impact & Interpretation. The intertextuality between these characters demonstrates memetic seriality and character-based humor's power to generate sustained narratives and social identification across distributed platforms.

11. Meme Economy

Origin & Background. The Meme Economy conceptualizes memes as tradable cultural capital, humorously assigning them market dynamics. It originated in forums and subreddits where users "buy" or "sell" memes metaphorically.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. This meta-meme functions as a playful critique of virality metrics, encouraging users to evaluate and forecast a meme's cultural trajectory. It frames memetic trends in terms of scarcity, novelty, and demand.

Impact & Interpretation. Meme Economy reveals an emergent reflexivity: communities not only produce content but also analyze and gamify its circulation. This reflexivity shapes creators' strategies and audience expectations around novelty and longevity.

12. Sheesh

Origin & Background. "Sheesh" is an elongation of an exclamation used to convey astonishment or approval, popularized on short-form platforms with accompanying gestures or audio cues. It spread rapidly through performative videos.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. The meme signals emphasis—often ironic—on trivial or unexpected achievements. Its performative kinesthetics (hand gestures, vocal tone) make it a compelling multimodal marker for shared affect.

Impact & Interpretation. The adoption of "Sheesh" reflects the interplay of prosody and gesture in memetic evolution: short verbal exclamations gain memetic power when paired with distinctive embodied performance, fostering imitation and remix.

13. Sussy / Among Us

Origin & Background. Stemming from the multiplayer game Among Us, "sussy" (suspicious) entered vernacular languages as a shorthand for distrust. The term circulated widely during the game's popularity peak and persisted in meme culture.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. "Sussy" functions as playful accusation and social signaling in contexts far removed from gaming, applied to dubious behavior or questionable assertions. The meme demonstrates linguistic uptake from games to broader social uses.

Impact & Interpretation. The Among Us lexicon illustrates how interactive game mechanics inform conversational tropes and memetic vocabulary, bridging participatory play with everyday evaluative language.

14. Eboy / Egirl Aesthetics

Origin & Background. Eboy and egirl aesthetics emerged from fashion subcultures and social media visual practices, later becoming a subject of satire and memeification. These visual tropes—dark clothes, layered accessories, and stylized makeup—circulated as both style and caricature.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. Memes about eboy/egirl aesthetics oscillate between critique and celebration, often exaggerating features to comment on performative online identities. Such memes operate as shorthand for particular generational or subcultural alignments.

Impact & Interpretation. Their memetic treatment highlights how aesthetics become semantically charged: visual signifiers carry identity work, and their parody reveals tensions around authenticity, commodification, and self-presentation online.

15. Corecore

Origin & Background. Corecore denotes a collage-style meme that blends disparate imagery and audio to evoke mood rather than straightforward comedy. It developed among creators seeking expressive, often melancholic, montage aesthetics.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. Corecore functions as aesthetic commentary: creators curate evocative juxtapositions to reflect contemporary anxieties and cultural dissonance. Its appeal lies in abstraction and emotional resonance rather than punchlines.

Impact & Interpretation. The format blurs lines between art and meme, prompting scholarly interest in how memetic forms can produce affective critique and collective mood-making, particularly among younger digital cohorts.

16. Fall Guys Gameplay Clips

Origin & Background. Clips from chaotic multiplayer games like Fall Guys became memetic due to their slapstick unpredictability. Short recordings of improbable outcomes are prime material for shareable humor.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. These clips celebrate emergent play and communal amusement at failure or serendipity; they are shared as exemplars of casual competitive chaos and reflexive entertainment.

Impact & Interpretation. Game-clip memes foreground participatory spectatorship, where viewers enjoy both the play and the meta-commentary, reinforcing community affiliation around common gaming experiences.

17. No Nut November & Challenge Memes

Origin & Background. Challenge memes like No Nut November leverage communal commitment to a task (often humorous or ironic) across a set timeframe. They spread via forums, social feeds, and dedicated challenge communities.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. These memes create ritualized participation, using humor and peer accountability to encourage engagement. Their structure encourages documentation, boasting, and satire, fueling continued circulation.

Impact & Interpretation. Such phenomena illustrate how social incentives and gamification dynamics structure memetic participation, converting private practices into public spectacle and community-building exercises.

18. AI-Generated Meme Remixes

Origin & Background. With advances in generative models, creators began producing surreal or uncanny meme content algorithmically, remixing images, captions, and styles at scale. These outputs rapidly entered meme pools, often as novelties.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. AI-generated memes are used for absurdist humor, commentary on automated creativity, and experimentation with forms that would be difficult to produce manually. They quickly became a tool for avant-garde memetics.

Impact & Interpretation. These remixes provoke reflection on authorship, originality, and the technological mediation of cultural production. They also highlight ethical and aesthetic debates about machine-generated humor in public discourse.

19. Doge v. Cheems Multiverse

Origin & Background. The narrative expansion of Doge and Cheems into multiverse formats extends character-based memetics into serialized storytelling. Creators craft alternate scenarios that reframe persona dynamics across contexts.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. These multiverse memes employ episodic logic to generate sustained engagement, leveraging character continuity to explore identity, irony, and interpersonal contrast across iterations.

Impact & Interpretation. The practice demonstrates meme culture’s capacity to develop persistent, evolving mythologies; fan-driven iterations produce layered intertextuality reminiscent of serialized popular culture.

20. Viral TikTok Challenges

Origin & Background. TikTok challenges synthesize audio, choreography, and visual editing into reproducible formats; they spread rapidly due to algorithmic amplification and ease of replication.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. Challenges operate as mechanisms of participatory culture, enabling broad creativity under constrained templates and providing visibility to creators who rework the same core elements.

Impact & Interpretation. Their dominance exemplifies platform-driven memetic evolution: algorithmic curation, social affordances, and cultural timing interact to generate intense short-lived waves of shared behavior and aesthetic practice.

21. Distracted Boyfriend

Origin & Background. The "Distracted Boyfriend" stock photo became memetic through its clear triangular narrative, enabling creators to label each figure and produce countless relational jokes. It achieved rapid international circulation as an adaptable template.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. The image serves as a vehicle for commentary about shifting attention, priorities, or loyalties—be they personal, political, or economic. Its readability makes it ideal for satire and point-driven jokes.

Impact & Interpretation. The meme’s lifecycle illustrates how stock imagery can gain new semiotic life; scholarly attention has focused on issues of authorship, the transformation of commercial content into communal humor, and the ethics of reuse.

22. Loss

Origin & Background. "Loss" refers to a four-panel comic whose layout became a shorthand for melodramatic or unexpected tragedy; it mutated into an abstracted meme where even minimal references invoke the original narrative.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. The meme’s evolution into an inside joke demonstrates memetic abstraction: users encode complex references into minimalist symbols, rewarding insider comprehension and remix ingenuity.

Impact & Interpretation. "Loss" exemplifies how narrative specificity can be obliquely preserved in memetic shorthand, raising questions about referential opacity and the social dynamics of shared cultural literacy online.

23. Roll Safe

Origin & Background. The "Roll Safe" image—depicting a man tapping his temple—became a visual shorthand for smug or facetious "clever" thinking, adapted widely for ironic guidance or faux wisdom.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. Creators deploy the template to satirize flawed reasoning or to present humorous pseudo-solutions. Its direct body language communicates mock-intelligence succinctly and effectively.

Impact & Interpretation. The meme reflects the potency of embodied gesture as rhetorical shorthand and demonstrates how single frames can be re-semantized into performative critique across cultural contexts.

24. Woman Yelling at Cat

Origin & Background. This composite juxtaposes a screaming woman and an unimpressed cat at a dinner table; its contrast incited layered captions and dialogic humor, quickly becoming a popular meme template.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. The format sets up opposing perspectives, often dramatizing conflict with absurdist contrast. Users create comedic pairings of human agitation and animal indifference to highlight mismatched reactions.

Impact & Interpretation. The meme demonstrates how juxtaposition and intertextual pairing enable rapid comedic framing and invites commentary on performative emotiveness versus stoic nonchalance.

25. "This is Fine"

Origin & Background. Derived from a comic of a dog in a burning room calmly stating "This is fine," the image became shorthand for denial or ironic calm in the face of disaster.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. Creators use the panel to comment on environmental, political, or personal crises where denial or complacency is observed, creating a wry, melancholic humor.

Impact & Interpretation. The meme's wide resonance highlights cultural mechanisms of coping through irony; it crystallizes existential faux-composure as a memeable, reflective mode of social critique.

26. Moth Lamp

Origin & Background. The "Moth Lamp" meme juxtaposes images of moths with lamp adulation, often overlaid with absurd captions; it plays on the moth's instinctual attraction to light and exaggerates it for humor.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. It operates as a playful comment on irresistible desires or compulsive behaviors, humorously equating stimulus-response patterns with human tendencies.

Impact & Interpretation. Moth Lamp demonstrates how animal behavior can be anthropomorphized into metaphorical commentary, using natural imagery to reflect cultural compulsions in a lighthearted way.

27. "OK Boomer"

Origin & Background. "OK Boomer" originated as a retort to perceived intergenerational condescension and quickly became a succinct dismissal of outdated attitudes. It consolidated as a meme during intergenerational discourse spikes.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. The phrase encapsulates generational tension, functioning both as a humorous comeback and as a rhetorical boundary signaling ideological difference. It circulates across social and political contexts.

Impact & Interpretation. As a memetic artifact, "OK Boomer" reveals how short utterances can crystallize socio-cultural critique, enabling rapid expression of generational divergence and prompting academic interest in discourse and identity politics.

28. Big Chungus

Origin & Background. Big Chungus features an exaggeratedly rotund Bugs Bunny image labeled humorously; it gained traction for the sheer incongruity of the inflated cartoon figure presented as an "epic" subject.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. The meme's absurdism and playful hyperbole allow it to be repurposed as mock-epic praise or as a vehicle for parodying fandom intensity and meme commodification.

Impact & Interpretation. Big Chungus highlights how decontextualized cartoon images can be refashioned into iconographic memes that mock their own memetic worth, reflecting self-aware humor about meme culture itself.

29. Mocking SpongeBob

Origin & Background. Mocking SpongeBob uses a still of SpongeBob bent and mimicking speech to indicate sarcastic repetition. It rose to popularity via its immediate visual cue for mocking tone.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. People apply the image to signify derision, satirizing statements by repeating them in a distorted manner. The format's exaggerated posture immediately signals ridicule.

Impact & Interpretation. This meme illustrates the power of body language in visual media to convey complex paralinguistic meaning and underscores how parodic mimicry functions as a core mechanism of internet satire.

30. Surprised Pikachu

Origin & Background. Surprised Pikachu—a screencap of an open-mouthed Pikachu—became emblematic of feigned astonishment or mock surprise; it is routinely used to highlight predictable consequences treated as unexpected.

Cultural Meaning & Usage. The meme serves as sarcastic commentary, indicating that an outcome should have been anticipated. Its broad recognizability allows quick, ironic reframing of cause-and-effect scenarios.

Impact & Interpretation. Surprised Pikachu's endurance testifies to how mass-mediated children's media supplies enduring symbolic assets for online discourse, enabling compact expressions of incredulous irony in public conversation.
